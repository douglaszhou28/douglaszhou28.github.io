About Doug

Why Language?

To put it simply, influenced by Plato's works early on, my life goal is to seek the truth.

Life is too short to study everything, so I decided to focus on the one thing I find most promising to break the black box of human intelligence: language.

My love for language comes from two different worlds. On one side, inspired by philosophy (especially Wittgenstein's work), I realized that language shapes how we think and see reality. We are truly living in a world built of language. On the other side, the sudden rise of Generative AI makes us wonder: what does it actually mean to "understand" something? To me, language is the definitive boundary where the mind meets the world.

What makes language uniquely fascinating is its role as an intellectual pivot. It sits perfectly at the intersection of the humanities and hard sciences. Through the lens of language, I can easily interface with cognitive neuroscience, psychology, NLP/AI, logic, philosophy, and even art.

In my future research, I want to bring two fields together: Cognitive Science (using scientific approaches like behavioral experiments and brain imaging to study how language works) and Philosophy (thinking deeply about the mind and science itself). Science without philosophy can lack depth, while philosophy without science can lack evidence. By bringing them together, I believe we can get closer to the truth.

Hobbies

Beyond the lab, I am a consumer of stories and ideas. I enjoy reading philosophy and literature, watching films, practicing Chinese calligraphy, and long-distance running. If you also happen to appreciate Plato, Hume, Wittgenstein, Calvino, Faulkner, García Márquez, or if you love the films of Ryusuke Hamaguchi, Eric Rohmer, Edward Yang, and Jacques Tati, please reach out and let's have a chat!

I also write and run a WeChat official account, where I regularly publish my thoughts, poems, prose, and fiction. For me, engaging with language is not just a research career, but a way of living.
