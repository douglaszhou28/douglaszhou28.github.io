Hi, welcome to my webpage!

I am Douglas Zhou (you can also call me Doug), a senior undergraduate majoring in Psychology at Zhejiang University, China (QS global ranking: 47th). I am expected to graduate in June 2027 and am actively seeking Ph.D. opportunities in Cognitive Neuroscience, Psychology or Linguistics for Fall 2027.

During my undergrad, I collaborate closely with Prof. Xiang-Zhen Kong at the Cognomics Lab, where I lead an fMRI project investigating the heterogeneity of sentence comprehension and how the brain integrates diverse cognitive dimensions during reading. Additionally, I serve as a part-time Research Assistant at the Play and Learning Lab (PI: Prof. Danyang Han), gaining hands-on experience in infant behavioral experiments.

Currently, I am a Visiting Research Assistant at the Neurolinguistics Lab (NeLLab) at New York University, working with Prof. Liina Pylkkänen to study the capacity limitations of human language processing using MEG.
